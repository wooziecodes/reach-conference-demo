Financial Literacy for Migrant Workers Through Conversational AI

CASE STUDY OVERVIEW

Singapore's 1.59 million migrant workers play a vital role in sustaining the nation's infrastructure, yet many spend their rare rest days queueing at banks for basic financial tasks.

This reveals a gap in accessibility and digital inclusion. Although most workers own smartphones, many are unfamiliar with digital tools, presenting an opportunity to design AI-driven solutions.

Our project explores how co-created chatbots can make financial literacy support more accessible and approachable, helping migrant workers manage their finances more efficiently and meaningfully.

ACTIONABLE INSIGHTS
  • Meet people where they are: design co-creation activities that align with their contexts and realities, not ours
  • Importance of accessibility and personalisation: account for diverse literacy levels, languages, and individual financial goals
  • Family as motivation: recognize that supporting family drives migrant workers' financial decisions
  • Build genuine relationships: trust and rapport are essential to elicit meaningful input and ensure solutions are relevant
