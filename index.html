Financial AI for Migrant Workers

Empowering Financial Inclusion Through Voice Technology

The Technical Trap We Fell Into

We'll be honest — typical tech hubris:
"Slap GPT-4 on it, write a prompt, ship it."
Our First Version: "Save $50 this month"
↓ Technically impressive, instant personalized advice ↓
But completely disconnected...
"After I send money home and pay for food and transport, I don't even have that much left."
— Worker feedback
We optimized for technical elegance but built for the wrong model:
Individual optimization when we should have designed for collectivist family systems

What We Learned

🎭 The Script Paradox

Workers type casually ("how much") but want formal responses. What looks like a UX quirk is actually about respect and dignity in financial advice.

🌐 Same Workplace, Different Worlds

Two workers at SMU had vastly different financial realities. Within-group diversity requires personalization, not population-level assumptions.

⏱️ The Latency Problem

A 10-15 second delay isn't just latency — it's a worker on lunch break giving up because the tool doesn't respect their time.

You can't API-call your way to equity.
